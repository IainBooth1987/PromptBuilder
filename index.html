Section 1 — The Framework

The 6-Part Prompt

Every part matters. The more context you give, the better the output. These six parts apply to both teaching resources and professional tasks.

1
🎭
Part 1 · Role
Who is the AI?
Set the AI's persona and expertise — primes vocabulary, depth, and pedagogical framing.
"You are an expert A-Level Economics teacher with 15 years of UK classroom experience..."
2
👥
Part 2 · Audience
Who are they for?
Year group, ability, prior knowledge — or for admin tasks, the intended reader of the document.
"My pupils are Year 10, mixed ability. They understand supply and demand but not yet PED."
3
🎯
Part 3 · Objective
What's the goal?
The specific outcome — learning goal, document purpose, or what the reader must be able to do.
"Pupils must calculate PED, interpret the coefficient, and predict the effect on total revenue."
4
⚡
Part 4 · Task
What to produce?
Name the exact output — worksheet, mark scheme, report comment, parent letter, agenda.
"Create a differentiated worksheet: retrieval starter, two-level main task, extension challenge."
5
🔒
Part 5 · Constraints
What are the limits?
Length, reading age, UK English, tone, excluded content. Negative constraints are the most powerful.
"Max 2 sides of A4. UK examples only. Reading age 14. No colour printing assumed."
6
📄
Part 6 · Format
How should it look?
Plain text, table, two-column, or a downloadable interactive file. Be specific about structure.
"Plain text for Word. Pupil version first, teacher key clearly separated. Flag [CHECK THIS] where uncertain."

Section 3 — Getting Better Results

Prompt Pro Tips

Six habits that separate good AI outputs from great ones — regardless of which tool you use.

🔄
Iterate — don't restart
Continue the conversation. "Make the starter easier", "add 3 more questions", "change the tone". Each refinement builds without losing context.
📏
Be precise about length
Vague prompts produce vague outputs. "Exactly 8 questions", "no more than 2 sides of A4", "each comment 50–70 words".
🎯
Paste in spec language
Include assessment objective wording directly — or use the Spec panel in the builder. The AI mirrors the exact vocabulary pupils need in exams.
❌
Use "do not" instructions
"Do not use American English", "do not use US companies", "do not pad with generic openers." Negative constraints are often the most powerful part.
📋
Ask for misconceptions
End nearly every resource prompt with: "List the 3 most common pupil misconceptions and how to address each." Huge value at no extra cost.
✅
Always review before use
The builder asks the AI to mark uncertain content [CHECK THIS]. AI can be confidently wrong. Read the full output before sharing with pupils or parents.
